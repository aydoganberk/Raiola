## North Star

- Title: `Analytics Platform with Operator Dense discipline`
- Promise: Data-heavy products need dense but legible hierarchy, evidence rails, and stable state design.
- Execution bias: Build toward Linear Precision structure + VoltAgent Command Energy type cues + VoltAgent Command Energy accent restraint.
- Constraint: Use the control-plane archetype as the shell rule and Operator Dense as the tie-breaker when multiple UI directions seem valid.

## Reference Blend

- Structure lead: `Linear Precision`
- Typography lead: `VoltAgent Command Energy`
- Accent lead: `VoltAgent Command Energy`
- Prefer Linear Precision for hierarchy, rails, density, and section framing.
- Borrow type rhythm and voice cues from VoltAgent Command Energy without cloning the entire brand.

- Merge rule: When references disagree, keep Operator Dense clarity and analytics platform usability above stylistic novelty.

## Reference Presets

### Linear Precision

- Source: [awesome-design-md / linear.app](https://github.com/VoltAgent/awesome-design-md/tree/main/design-md/linear.app)
- Signature: Precise hierarchy, thin borders, disciplined density, and confident restraint.
- Why chosen: Linear Precision matched the current product category, taste, or archetype signals.
- Adopt: Use crisp containment and obvious hierarchy instead of oversized chrome.
- Adopt: Keep operational lists, tables, and command surfaces tightly aligned.
- Adopt: Let layout rhythm and state clarity do more work than decorative styling.
- Avoid: Do not flood the UI with colorful badges or heavy gradients.
- Avoid: Do not replace high-signal list/table patterns with oversized marketing cards.

### VoltAgent Command Energy

- Source: [awesome-design-md / voltagent](https://github.com/VoltAgent/awesome-design-md/tree/main/design-md/voltagent)
- Signature: Dark command-center focus, signal-color discipline, and architecture-first storytelling.
- Why chosen: VoltAgent Command Energy matched the current product category, taste, or archetype signals.
- Adopt: Use one signal accent color with discipline instead of a rainbow of status hues.
- Adopt: Let code, workflow diagrams, or architecture content act as hero material when relevant.
- Adopt: Use dark surfaces only when they strengthen focus and contrast, not as a default trend choice.
- Avoid: Do not turn every surface into a glowing cyberpunk panel.
- Avoid: Do not use generic blue enterprise chrome when the product needs stronger technical character.

### Cohere Data-Rich

- Source: [awesome-design-md / cohere](https://github.com/VoltAgent/awesome-design-md/tree/main/design-md/cohere)
- Signature: Enterprise credibility, data-forward composition, and restrained gradient energy.
- Why chosen: Cohere Data-Rich matched the current product category, taste, or archetype signals.
- Adopt: Use clear section framing so sophisticated product claims stay legible.
- Adopt: Reserve richer color or gradient moments for one or two intentional emphasis surfaces.
- Adopt: Make proof and product explanation feel structured rather than decorative.
- Avoid: Do not let gradient energy overpower readability or trust.
- Avoid: Do not mix too many accent colors across the same screen family.

## Anti-Pattern Bans

- Do not replace data relationships with oversized decorative cards.
- Do not bury status, risk, or primary actions below fold-heavy hero chrome.
- Do not mix multiple visual metaphors on one screen.
- Do not hide core actions in tertiary menus when the task is frequent.
- Do not use color as the only state signal.
- Do not add gradients/shadows/radii without a consistent token story.
- Do not let loading/empty/error states regress behind the happy path.
- Do not waste vertical space with oversized chrome.
- Avoid decorative gradients that reduce scan speed.
- Avoid oversized cards that waste vertical space and slow operator scanning.
- Do not flood the UI with colorful badges or heavy gradients.
- Do not replace high-signal list/table patterns with oversized marketing cards.
- Do not turn every surface into a glowing cyberpunk panel.
- Do not use generic blue enterprise chrome when the product needs stronger technical character.
- Do not let gradient energy overpower readability or trust.
- Do not mix too many accent colors across the same screen family.

## Codex Rules

- Start from Linear Precision hierarchy before adding expressive details.
- Use Operator Dense tokens and analytics platform expectations as the decision rule for new components.
- Reference Linear Precision, VoltAgent Command Energy, Cohere Data-Rich for composition cues, not for literal cloning.
- Patch loading, empty, error, and success coverage in the same pass as visual polish whenever possible.
- If the screen starts to look like a generic template, tighten type rhythm, section hierarchy, and proof surfaces before adding new effects.
